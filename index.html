About

A reporter's eye, a storyteller's heart

Kieran Devaney has spent a lifetime telling other people's stories — and, eventually, a few of his own. Over a thirty-year career in television and print journalism he reported and produced programmes for TV-am, Channel 4, ITN and Sky News in the United Kingdom, CNN in the United States, and TV3 in Ireland, where he produced The Vincent Browne Programme until his retirement from daily broadcasting.

His writing has appeared in The London Times and in publications around the world, while his documentary work — more than 100 films made across three decades — earned him a Sony Award and two Gold Medals at the New York International Festival of Radio and Television. His reporting took him into the field as well as the studio: he covered Burma's 1990 election and Aung San Suu Kyi's struggle for democracy from inside the country, alongside Karen freedom fighters.

In 2009 he left Dublin for Rosses Point, County Sligo, to write full time. He now publishes novels rooted in the landscape, folklore and history around him, leads guided tours through Rosses Point's seafaring and literary heritage, and still writes and broadcasts on the stories that matter to him.

Documentary Spotlight

The Last King of Ireland

RTÉ Radio 1 · Documentary on One · 2018

For two and a half years, Kieran recorded the life of his close friend Patsy Dan Rodgers, King of Tory Island — a title with an unbroken lineage back to the fifth century. Made with co-producer Tim Desmond, the documentary is an intimate portrait of a man defending a vanishing way of life on a remote Atlantic island while confronting his own mortality.

It stands among the more than 100 documentaries Kieran has made over his career — one built on gaining the trust of remarkable people and staying with their stories long enough to do them justice.

Beyond the Page

Rosses Point & Yeats Country

Kieran now leads guided tours through Rosses Point's seafaring and literary heritage — the harbour, the pilots' houses, and the shadow of Ben Bulben and Drumcliffe that shaped W.B. Yeats. It is the same landscape and history that runs through his own novels.
